Rules - Dos & Don'ts
DO
  1. Ensure your templates are up to date by using the download link below
  2. Use the Terms and conditions apply layer found in the PSD template
  3. Ensure you follow the toolkit guidelines and only use the toolkit elements that are available in the PSD
  4. Submitted your artwork in .PNG/.JPEG/.JPG at 72ppi - Asda will not be able to change your artwork once submitted
  5. Ensure your artwork is accessible and meets our Accessibility Requirements
DON'T
  1. Don't resize banners or change the PPI
  2. Don't create and use your own new roundels in our template
  3. Don't use your own "terms and conditions apply" small print in our template
  4. Don't provide terms and conditions in tables, only in text format
  5. Don't include text to win phone numbers or URLs on driving media - only on the branded page/competition headers
Accessibility

In accordance with government accessibility requirements all content featured on the Asda website must meet level AA of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG 2.1) as a minimum.

Therefore we require all artwork sent to Asda to comply with the guidelines below. Failure to comply with these guidelines could lead to a rejection of artwork and a delay to campaign live dates.


Color Contrast
Text on artwork must comply with the WCAG AA standards (you can check your artwork using this tool)
Font Family & Size
The font should be clear and readable for all users, this includes the typeface and the font size. We recommend at least 12pt in Source Sans Pro - Semibold for important information.
Background
The background shouldn't interfere with accessibility of the text. Make sure to use solid backgrounds for blocks of text, use thick text outlines, and use strong colour contrast.
